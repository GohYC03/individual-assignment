What is an Esports Commentator?

An esports commentator (sometimes called a shoutcaster) provides live narration and analysis during competitive gaming events. Much like traditional sports commentators, their job is to energize the broadcast, break down strategies, and help viewers understand the action.

There are typically three main types of esports commentary:

  • Play-by-Play: Describes the real-time action as it unfolds, focusing on speed and accuracy.
  • Color Commentary: Adds context, background stories, and in-depth analysis to enhance the broadcast.
  • Hype/Shout Casting: Brings excitement and energy, especially during intense or unexpected moments.

Esports commentators keep viewers engaged by mixing live updates, expert insights, and high-energy reactions, making competitive gaming events more accessible and thrilling for audiences worldwide.

A Brief History of Esports and Commentary

The roots of esports trace back to the early 1970s, when students at Stanford University held a tournament for the video game Spacewar!. The 1980s saw larger competitions like the Space Invaders Championship, which attracted over 10,000 participants. As PC gaming rose in the 1990s, titles such as Doom, Quake, and StarCraft became staples of competitive play, with professional leagues like Major League Gaming (MLG) emerging in the early 2000s.

The 2010s marked a turning point with the rise of streaming platforms like Twitch, allowing fans to watch tournaments and live commentary from anywhere in the world. Today, esports is a multi-billion dollar industry, with commentators playing a crucial role in bringing the excitement and strategy of games to a global audience.
